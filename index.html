A Single Dose of Psilocybin Physically Rewires the Brain, and the Changes Last a Month

UCSF / Nature Communications, May 2026

Researchers gave 28 psychedelic-naive volunteers one 25mg dose. Within 60 minutes, EEG showed dramatically higher brain entropy. A month later, DTI scans showed their neural tracts were physically denser with more structural integrity. The degree of entropy during the experience predicted how much insight and well-being each person reported weeks later. First study showing lasting anatomical brain changes from a single dose in healthy people.

Christof Koch: "The Brain Might Not Create Consciousness After All"

BIAL Foundation Symposium, April 2026

Koch, the former chief scientist of the Allen Institute for Brain Science, argued consciousness is an intrinsic property of any sufficiently complex integrated system, not a product of the brain. He outlined three areas where materialist models fail: the impossibility of fully reducing subjective experience to neural processes, the difficulty modern physics faces in defining what is "real," and the persistence of mystical and near-death experiences that resist conventional explanation.

Seven Days of Meditation Rewires the Brain to Resemble Psychedelic States

Communications Biology / UC San Diego, April 2026

Twenty adults completed a 7-day residential retreat with 33 hours of guided practice. Brain scans showed reduced default mode network chatter, boosted immune signaling, increased natural pain-relief chemicals. The striking finding: participants who reported stronger mystical experiences showed brain states resembling those seen under psychedelics. The convergence between meditation-induced and psychedelic-induced brain states, achieved entirely without drugs.

Virtual Trial Simulates Psychedelics for Patients with Disorders of Consciousness

Advanced Science / Wiley, 2026

Computational simulations of LSD and psilocybin in vegetative and minimally conscious patients showed psychedelics shifted brain activity toward "criticality," the edge-of-chaos state associated with healthy conscious processing. The effect was stronger in minimally conscious patients. This opens the idea that psychedelics might literally reboot consciousness in brain-injured patients. Not yet tested in humans, but the theoretical framework is compelling.

Adults with Autism Show Parkinson's-Like Brain Abnormalities

UC Davis MIND Institute, May 2026

Brain imaging reveals young adults with autism show scan abnormalities typically associated with Parkinson's disease, opening an unexpected connection and a potential path toward early prevention. Separately, researchers found the MDGA1 gene mutation causes autism primarily in males, while females are protected by estrogen, which may explain the long-observed gender disparity in autism diagnosis. The Parkinson's-like finding suggests monitoring and intervention strategies could be borrowed from Parkinson's research.
